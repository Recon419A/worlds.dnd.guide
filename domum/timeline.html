History of Domum

  • Gods of the Void create the Universe

    Beginning of the Universe

    The entites of unimaginable power which exist in the beyond accidentally create the universe. This new form of existence is wholly incompatible with them, causing them to either go insane or retreat deep into the void, never to be seen again.

  • Matter is Created

    Before Time

    A newborn void god enters reality, thinking he can withstand it. He was wrong. The sheer force of the change causes him to explode, and his remains coalesced into solid matter and energy.

  • The Planet Forms

    Before Life

    Space dust coalesces into stars and planets. Domum is formed, and as the planet's rotation begins to slow, it cools. Water begins pooling on the surface.

  • The beginning of an Era

    Age of the Woods

    Plants formed first. Within five hundred thousand years of the cooling, collosal trunks stemmed from the ground, moving as slowly as icebergs, ripping up the terrain. Over billions of years, they created the continents, and the mountains and valleys, and tectonic plates through their trudging along through the ground.

  • Fauna Develop

    Age of the Woods

    Some of the smaller trees uproot themselves and walk around faster, a distinct evolutionary advantage.

  • First Extinction

    Age of the Woods

    The planet is pelted by an onslaught of asteroids, the impact of which caused detritus to blot out the sun and sky, causing worldwide extinction of the tree colossi.

  • A New Age

    Age of Dusk

    With the sun gone, the colossi roaming the barely-formed world died out. Temperatures rose and fell unpredictably due to the impenetrable, debris-covered sky. It was into this the surviving organisms evolved. The organisms' skin changed from wood to stone or jagged metal, barring those in certain, well-preserved areas, which preserved their wooden skin. These proto-demons reproduced when a bit of their core - essentially a hard, rock-like proto-brain - chipped off and assembled itself into it's own shell. The size of their body was dependent on the size of their core, which animated the stone/metal that made up their body. The being would consume energy of whatever form it was formed from to increase it's size.
    From this evolutionary mechanic different forms of demon appeared, ranging from the smallest (imps) to the largest (leviathans).

  • Biting Frost and Singing Heat

    Age of Dusk

    Those beings which survived on land learned to feed on the temperatures surrounding them, molding them into the frost and flame demons, respectively. These large beings were quite fearsome, with their animalistic stone and metal carapaces streaming fire or frost from every chip, crack, and hole in their hard carapaces.

  • Shining Poison

    Age of Dusk

    Those which retreated into the oceans had much different evolutions. They grew strong shell-like flesh, and those who dwelled in the far deep grew sharp, erratic crystal skin. Those near the surface absorbed the debris that rained down from the sky and began to excrete poisons and toxins, and some even grew parasitic. The deep dwellers became most intelligent, though still animals: they learned to manipulate light and pressure, creating strong illusions to attract their prey.

  • Hunting in Packs

    Age of Dusk

    Over thousands of years, tiny evolutions in intelligence led to the first primitive society among the land-dwelling demons. They were little more than packs, which were at their core simple non-agression agreements in order to make hunting prey easier for all involved.

  • The Packs Evolve

    Age of Dusk

    Hundreds of thousands of years later, the pack mentality evolved into what could be called a proto-civilization. Still lacking a language or reasonable technology, members of packs began to specialize themselves, some hunting and others raising young, which was a brand new development.

  • True Civilization

    Age of Dusk

    Millenia pass, and evolution favored the beings with larger brains. At this time land demons are intelligent enough to form a primitive language, and small settlements in caves and under crags. Fights regularly broke out between rival settlements. It is also at this time that the aquatic demons begin to form packs and proto-societies of their own, yet it will be another hundred thousand years before they reach the level of the land-dwellers.

  • The Packs Evolve

    Age of Dusk

    Hundreds of thousands of years later, the pack mentality evolved into what could be called a proto-civilization. Still lacking a language or reasonable technology, members of packs began to specialize themselves, some hunting and others raising young, which was a brand new development.

  • Demons Reign

    Age of Night

    Over time, the planet's sky clears, and the demons begin to proliferate faster, signalling the start of the Age of Night. The power of the sun strengthened the heat demons, who forced the remaining frost demons into slavery. It was also at this time that the Toxin Demons and Illusion Demons of the oceans began a rivalry, preventing as much scientific progress as the land demons, who had invented primitive tools.

  • Hell's Mines

    Age of Night

    The discovery of metalworking led to more durable tools, and a need for more metals. The enslaved frost demons were forced to dig deep, over the years creating a network of mines spanning most of the planet's underground.

  • Revolution

    Age of Night

    The frost demons had been forced to dig mines so deep they stumbled upon a rare deep metal ore: A bone-white metal called icesteel, or "Dlarun" in the Demon Language. This frosty, heat-nullifying metal became a source of strength to the frost demons, who eventually began to form their bodies of it. This gave them a powerful advantage against the heat demons, and they revolted, forming their own civilizations at the planet's poles.

  • The Ether Demons Rise

    Age of Night

    Over time, the crystal demons assimilated the poison demons to give rise to a new race: The Ether Demons. They wielded the powers of illusion, pressure, and toxins to form a new substance entirely: Ether. They were able to utilize the ether to effectively survive on land, leading them into contact with the other demonic species, and were eventually dragged into the war between the frost and fire demons.

  • Discovery of the Aegis Flame

    Age of Night

    Thousands of years pass. War still rages between the flame and frost demons, seemingly without end. It only stopped when a truly special thing was found by a young group of three outcasts who banded together for survival: the flame demon Asmodeus, the frost demon Wendigo, and the Ether demon Axii. They had found a primordial energy which became known as the aegis flame. Unable to attune itself to all their distinct auras at once, the flame split into three, giving each the power of a deity and making them the first gods.
